Milk is a nutrient fluid produced by the mammary glands of female mammals during lactation. The natural purpose of milk is to feed [1] offspring (including humans), which are not yet capable of digesting other food. Milk is now a component of many products used by humans, and its production has become a major industry. The technical regulation defines milk as a product of normal physiological secretion of the mammary glands of farm animals, obtained from one or more animals during lactation during one or more milking, without any additions to this product
